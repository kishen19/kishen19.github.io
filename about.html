Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore

Summer, 2019

Narendra Summer Research Intern

In this project, I studied the 2D Strip Packing problem under the guidance of Prof. Arindam Khan. The current best algorithm for this problem is a 5/3 + ε approximation algorithm, but with the running time heavily dependent on ε. The fastest algorithm is Steinberg's 2 approximation algorithm with a time complexity of O(nloglogn). Our goal was to develop a c-Approximation algorithm with running time O(nd), where c is strictly less than 2 and d is some reasonable constant (maybe ≤ 5).

We deeply analyzed Steinberg's and Schiermeyer's algorithms and developed insights on the problematic cases. We also devised some heuristics to deal with such cases. This project was a part of the Narendra Summer Internship Programme, IISc.
